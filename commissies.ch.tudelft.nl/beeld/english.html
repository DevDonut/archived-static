Christiaan Huygens unveiled - 30 May


On Friday the 30th of May, Student Society ‘Christiaan Huygens’ held a final, lasting event to mark the 50th anniversary it celebrated last year. A two-and-a-half meters tall statue of 17th century scientist Christiaan Huygens, forged by sculptor Martin Abspoel, was donated to the TU Delft to be placed in the new Mekelpark. Dean Professor Lenstra accepted the gift on behalf of the TU Delft and the faculty EEMCS. Besides this, the Christiaan Huygens Foundation, a charitable organisation, was founded to help with education in developing countries.



Despite efforts of the organisation to reveal the statue at its definitive location, the event had to take place in the faculty entrance hall. The statue will remain there until the foundation of the statue has been prepared. It still made a big impression, towering above all present. The revelation was proceeded by a lecture by Paul Burghardt of DECIS in lecture hall B



Left Martin Abspoel (artist/sculptor) and right Maarten
van der Beek (chairmen W.I.S.V 'Christiaan Huygens')
during the ceremony		     The lecture ended at 17.30 with drinks in the /Pub. Sandwiches and snacks were available so that students and staff didn’t have to worry about supper. After a while, the ceremony in the hallway began with a speech of the Dean, the chairman of Student Society ‘Christiaan Huygens’ Maarten van der Beek and Professor Vincent Icke: astrophysicist and very fascinated by the life and works of Christiaan Huygens

At 19.15, the honorary chairman, Professor Aarts, revealed the statue to the public and officially donated it to the faculty. Commemorative medals were handed out to all parties involved. Also, Martin Abspoel was presented as new member of achievement for his efforts.

After some words of thanks, the reception began and visitors could see the statue from up close. There were a lot of prominent alumni present, among others: founders of Exact Software and representatives of Corus and Microsoft.

The event ended with the lottery draw: two students and a member of the faculty staff went home with an X-Box 360 and two watches, donated by Microsoft and Delft Jewellery store Warnaar. The organisation would like to thank everyone involved in making this activity a great success: Martin Abspoel, the honorary members of the Student Society, Dean Professor Lenstra, MJ Vermeulen of Microsoft Netherlands, Kees-Jan Verstraten of Corus, Alumni society ‘Constantijn Huygens’, Rinus Dekker, Arco van Nieuwland, Paul van Keep, and all the others that contributed to the event and the foundation.

Christiaan Huygens Foundation

www.christiaanhuygens.org

Before the statue was revealed, the Dean announced the start of a new foundation. The Christiaan Huygens Foundation aims to improve the education for the less privileged around the world (mainly focussed on, but not limited to, Mathematics and Computer Science). It is an initiative to share the knowledge of the TU Delft, its students and their resourcefulness. This is to be achieved by stimulating and funding students taking action and setting up projects in these areas, funding projects, sending financial aid and/or resources. The foundation will be run entirely by students.
