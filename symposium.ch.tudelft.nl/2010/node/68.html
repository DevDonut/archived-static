Platform for Information Securtity

1. What is the PvIB?

The Platform for Information Security (PvIB) is the centre of expertise in the field of information security in the Netherlands. The PvIB is the platform where information, knowledge and experience about Information Security are collated, improved, enriched and in turn redistributed. The PvIB brings together all stakeholders and interested parties in the field of Information Security. The PvIB delivers a contribution to issues with a clear social relevance.

Organised by and for volunteers, the PvIB is a meeting point for professionals, including officers responsible for information security and all other persons who are interested, who wish to support one another through their expertise by exchanging knowledge and experience, on a friendly and voluntary basis. The platform also aims to promote expertise in the field of and increase awareness of information security.

2. Who are the members?

The members of the organisation represent a broad cross-section from the various areas of expertise and specialisation and from the wide range of target groups and sectors of industry. The PvIB is striving to manage the growth in the number of members to ensure that the friendly and voluntary character is maintained, while at the same time aiming for an age group distribution that ensures the continuity of membership.

3. What are our target groups?

The PvIB platform is intended for anyone interested in the field of information security. These are primarily Information Security professionals and IT auditors. In addition, there are professionals who include information security as part of their day-to-day work, and professionals employed in related fields such as:

  • (line)managers
  • CIOs
  • Risk & Compliancy managers
  • ICT administrators
  • Information architects
  • Management and ICT consultants
  • Business Continuity Managers
  • Data protection officers
  • Forensic IT investigators

Within the platform, members can organise themselves in subgroups. These groups may be classified according to level of experience, such as the Young Professionals, or professional position, for example professionals with a management role in information security. Within these subgroups, no differentiation is made according to age, level of education or sector in industry in which the professionals are employed.

4. What are we doing for Young Professionals?

Students and young professionals are an essential component of our target group. PvIB has entered into agreements with teaching institutes to ensure a low threshold for membership for students. Students are given the opportunity to come into contact with experienced professionals from the private sector and government, during PvIB meetings. This is clearly a win-win situation: encouraging interest in a dynamic professional field amongst young people with considerable up-to-date knowledge and with the best education, in consultation with experienced professionals, who thoroughly know the ropes in professional practice.

5. What do we do?

The PvIB focuses on three core products:

  • Activities, including theme-based meetings, the Information Security Consultation body (IBO) for management, Young Professionals, conferences and trade fairs. The annual Security Conference and the Joop Bautz Information Security Award (JBISA) strengthen the ties with related disciplines from associations in the field of information security. Through their physical presence, members are able to maintain and expand their network of professional relations.
  • Publications that support and promote further professionalisation, including the magazine ”Informatiebeveiling” (Information Security), the annual book Trends in Information Security, the Newsletter with news and information for members and media publications. We also have our own website www.pvib.nl
  • Professionalisation focused on knowledge development and best practices.
