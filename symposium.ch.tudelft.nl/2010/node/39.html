NGI, Platform for ICT-Professionals
The NGI offers a platform for ICT-professionals where the members can extend their knowledge and maintain their relations. The NGI is a guild for ICT-professionals, who are willing to share the full width of their experience and knowledge with other members, by which, at the same time, the individual specialism will be extended even further. It's a(inter)actieve society where connections and relations play an important part.

For whom can the NGI be of interest?

The NGI can be interesting for higher educated ICT-professionals in the Netherlands who are professionaly involved in ICT-management or education or apply ICT in a large-scale in their line of work. Its main purpose is the extension of knowledge and the interests in Computer Science in a broad view. This wide description enables us to keep organising new activities to try and keep up with the IT-(r)evolution. For a number of years now we see a development towards creating more application-targeted devisions. That's how new platforms in the form of new departments, associations, multidisciplenary workgroups etc., start. Every member is pushed to take the lead in these developments. The NGI can help you improve both socially as with respect to content: the NGI offers you an effective network for the versatile sharing of professional knowledge, experience and latest developments.

For Computer Science and Engineering students. Students can, through the NGI, link to real-world applications for -in example- final theses or internships.
Furthermore, the NGI employs a low fee for students, so that the possibilities for enrichment of one's education are within reach for every student. In short: with the NGI, you meet people from the line of work you study for!

Magazine "Informatie" and the "Informatie Scriptie prize"

The NGI is co-owner of the magazine Informatie. Informatie fulfulls an important part as a stage for publishing ICT-related articles. Also, the club-pages are used as a means of communcations for activities. Besides this, the NGI persues to stimluate usefull applications of computer technology and improve contacts between study-, faculty- and worksocieties by means of the Informatie Scriptie Prize. The rules concerning this prize are posted on our website.

Department the Hague

The department The Hague Region fullfills the networking function for the south-side of the "Randstad" and is know for its interesting lectures with informal getting-togehter afterwards.
These activities concentrate around the easy-to-reach location of the The Hague University (professional college), with excelent facilities. Since a couple of years, NGI department The Hague works together with Mathematics and Computer Science Student Society 'Christiaan Huygens' of the Delft University of Technology, in organising lectures and company visits for members and students.

For more information visit: www.ngi.nl.
