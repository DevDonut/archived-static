Symposium: Cloud your Identity, Distribute your Life

This April 27th, W.I.S.V. 'Christiaan Huygens' organises a symposium for Applied Mathematics and Computer Science students at Delft University of Technology. On this website you will find information about the symposium, and in a few months you can also buy your ticket here.

To stay informed about the symposium, you can subscribe to our newsletter in the menu on your left. For unanswered questions on the website, you van reach us at symposium@ch.tudelft.nl.

The goal of this symposium is to give an overview of recent developments in distributed systems, Peer-2-Peer collaboration and cryptology, and mainly how these topics intersect law processes and privacy issues. Therefore, the symposium will not only cover current scientific research, but also social and philosophical topics related to it.

Program and Forum update

The program schedule has undergone an update, you can find it here. Also the abstracts of the lectures are added and are available here.

Unfortunately Jérémie Zimmermann, as some might noticed at the Lecturers Page, is unable to attend the symposium due to his work for the European Union. The good news is that the lecture itself will remain the same thanks to dr. Philippe Aigrain, a colleague and co-founder of La Quadrature du Net, who will replace Jérémie Zimmermann.

At the forum we would like to start a discussion about privacy, law and ethics on certain topics. Dr.ir. Coen Vermeeren will be leading the discussions. The members of the panel will be announced later. We would like to thank Motiv for their advise and support which made this forum possible.
